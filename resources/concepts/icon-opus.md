# Icon concept — variant O (Opus 4.8)

**Concept: "Core & Ports."** The mark is a single IP-core module resting on the
warm brand tile: a rounded white block with three interface ports protruding
from each edge, its face carrying two rows of register/bitfield cells with one
field highlighted.

**Metaphor and read.** This is exactly how an IP core appears on the IP Core
canvas: a block you place and wire through connection points on its edges. The
protruding ports give a distinctive, un-chip-generic silhouette that says
"module with interfaces" (the primary pillar), while the knocked-out register
cells on the block face supply the Memory Map structure as a secondary read —
not a standalone register table like the old icon. The single amber field echoes
the previous icon's highlighted bitfield, preserving continuity while relegating
it to a detail.

**Legibility at ~40px.** Everything is built from bold filled forms, not
hairlines. The white module reads as one confident shape against the gradient;
the six ports keep the silhouette recognizable; and the amber field stays
visible as a warm spark down to 16px. The white-on-gradient contrast plus a
subtle lift shadow keeps it crisp on light, dark, and mid backgrounds.

**Color.** Kept the signature `#FF512F -> #DD2476` gradient — its warmth is real
brand equity and stands out in a sea of blue dev-tool icons — adding only one
analogous amber accent (`#FFC24A`) for the active bitfield.
